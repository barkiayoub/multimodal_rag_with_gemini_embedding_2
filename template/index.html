Executive Summary: Renewable Energy Trends 2024

Published: January 15, 2024

The global landscape for renewable energy has reached a pivotal inflection point. As of late 2023, installed solar capacity has surpassed 1.2 Terawatts globally, representing a 22% increase year-over-year. This growth is primarily driven by manufacturing efficiencies and supportive policy frameworks in major economies.

Wind energy continues to show robust performance, particularly in offshore installations. The average capacity factor for new offshore turbines has improved to 48%, making them increasingly competitive with traditional baseload power sources.

However, the integration of these intermittent sources remains a significant challenge for existing grid architectures. Investments in battery storage technologies and grid modernization are becoming as critical as the generation assets themselves.

Future projections suggest that by 2030, renewables will account for nearly 45% of total global electricity generation, provided that supply chain constraints for critical minerals like lithium and cobalt are effectively managed.
